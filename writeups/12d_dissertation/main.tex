Type IIB string theory possesses a conjectured non-perturbative $SL(2,\mathbb{Z})$ duality whose ten-dimensional origin remains unexplained. This thesis examines the proposal that the duality, and the theory itself, descend from a twelve-dimensional structure. We find that the $12$d interpretation is robust for the axio-dilaton and form-field sectors, but remains effective rather than fundamental.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Declaration of Contributions}
This thesis examines the proposal that the type IIB string theory has a twelve-dimensional origin, and the work presented in it is my own.
The greater parts of this thesis reviews existing literature, attributed by citation throughout: the survey of $10+2$d supergravity and superalgebras, the brane interpretations of the axio-dilaton, and the D3-brane worldvolume analysis report results due to others, principally A.~A.~Tseytlin and the works cited therein.

Two results are my own.
First, the covariant twelve-dimensional repackaging of the type IIB form-field sector in Chapter \ref{sec:covariant-unification}, in which the ten-dimensional self-duality of $F_5$ is realised as a twelve-dimensional Hodge duality.
Second, the result of Chapter \ref{sec:effective-actions} that the $U(1)$-preserving five-point contact term of \cite{Liu:2025uqu} admits no twelve-dimensional covariant form.
I performed these derivations and the supporting symbolic-algebra computations, and verified all results presented.
The synthesis of work scattered across the algebraic, brane, form-field, and effective-action perspectives into a single coherent account, and the line of argument running through the thesis, are also my own.
