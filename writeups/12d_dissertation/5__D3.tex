\section{The D3 Brane and Self-Duality}
\label{sec:d3-self-duality}
The low-energy dynamics of a D3 brane in a given type IIB supergravity background are described by the Dirac-Born-Infeld action supplemented by a Wess-Zumino term, with background metric, NSNS and RR fields entering through the appropriate pullbacks to the brane worldvolume \cite{Leigh:1989jq,Polchinski:1998rr}.
In particular, the bosonic sector of the D3 low-energy action takes the form \cite{Tseytlin:1996it}
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
S_{D3}
&=\int d^4x \sqrt{-\det\big({g}_{mn} + e^{-\Phi/2} (\mathcal{F}_2)_{mn}\big)}\\
&\quad+\int \Big(C_4 + C_2\wedge \mathcal{F}_2 + \tfrac{1}{2} C \mathcal{F}_2\wedge\mathcal{F}_2\Big)
+\text{higher order},\qquad
\mathcal{F}_2 \equiv dA+B_2 .
\end{aligned}
\label{D3 action}
\end{equation}
Throughout, the bulk supergravity fields are understood to be pulled back onto the brane worldvolume; $m,n$ label worldvolume indices, $(\mathcal{F}_2)_{mn}$ are the components of the worldvolume 2-form $\mathcal{F}_2$, and $C$ is the RR axion.
The vector $A_m$ is the dynamical gauge potential on the brane worldvolume.
It was shown \cite{Tseytlin:1996it} that the action is invariant only if the bulk background simultaneously undergoes an SL(2, Z) transformation; we derive this explicitly.
To quadratic order in $\mathcal F_2$ the gauge sector of \eqref{D3 action} reads, in form notation,
\begin{equation}
\int\left[\frac{e^{-\Phi}}{2}\mathcal F_2\wedge\star\mathcal F_2+\frac{C}{2}\mathcal F_2\wedge\mathcal F_2+C_2\wedge\mathcal F_2\right],\qquad \mathcal F_2 = dA+B_2,
\end{equation}
that is, Maxwell theory whose complexified coupling is the bulk axio-dilaton $\tau = C+ie^{-\Phi}$: the NSNS field $B_2$ enters through $\mathcal F_2$, and the RR field $C_2$ as a linear source.
To dualize, we promote $\mathcal F_2$ to an independent field and add a Lagrange multiplier 
\begin{equation}
\tilde{F}_2 \wedge (\mathcal{F}_2 - B_2)
\end{equation}
to enforce $d(\mathcal F_2-B_2) = 0$, where $\tilde F_2 = d\tilde A$ is the field strength of the dual potential $\tilde A$.
Varying the now-independent $\mathcal F_2$ instead gives the algebraic relation
\begin{equation}
e^{-\Phi}\star\mathcal F_2 + C\mathcal F_2 = -(\tilde F_2+C_2).
\label{D3-integrating-out-mathcal-F}
\end{equation}
We now integrate $\mathcal F_2$ out and ask whether the resultant D3 action is in the form \eqref{D3 action}, with $d\tilde A$ in place of $dA$. 
Using $\star^2=-1$, we have
\begin{equation}
(C+e^{-\Phi}\star)^{-1} = \frac{C-e^{-\Phi}\star}{C^2+e^{-2\Phi}},
\end{equation}
so \eqref{D3-integrating-out-mathcal-F} solves to $\mathcal F_2 = -\dfrac{C-e^{-\Phi}\star}{C^2+e^{-2\Phi}}(\tilde F_2+C_2)$. Substituting back, the gauge sector becomes the same Maxwell form for the dual field strength $\tilde F_2+C_2$, with the coupling inverted to $\tau'\equiv C'+ie^{-\Phi'}=-1/\tau$:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\int\bigg[&\frac{e^{-\Phi'}}{2}(\tilde F_2+C_2)\wedge\star(\tilde F_2+C_2)\\
&+\frac{C'}{2}(\tilde F_2+C_2)\wedge(\tilde F_2+C_2)-\tilde F_2\wedge B_2\bigg].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
We read off that this is in the same form as the D3 action \eqref{D3 action}, with $\tilde A$ in place of $A$, provided we make the following field redefinitions
\begin{equation}
\tau\to-\frac{1}{\tau},\qquad
B_2 \to C_2,\qquad C_2\to -B_2.
\end{equation}
This is precisely the $S$ generator of the type IIB bulk SL(2, Z) duality.
Because the D3 action is also invariant under the axion shift $C\to C+1$, the full SL(2, Z) duality is realized on the D3 worldvolume.

The nonlinear Born--Infeld terms do not affect this conclusion: the Born--Infeld determinant is invariant under the Legendre transform \cite{Tseytlin:1996it}, so the full action \eqref{D3 action} returns to its original form under this transformation.
The bulk generator $T: \tau\to \tau+1$ corresponds to $\theta\to \theta + 2\pi$ on the D3 worldvolume, for the complexified coupling $\tau_{YM}\equiv \frac{\theta}{2\pi} + \frac{4\pi i}{g^2}$.
Meanwhile, the bulk generator $S:\tau\to-1/\tau$ is mapped to the electromagnetic duality transformation on the worldvolume field strengths.
Thus the SL(2, Z) duality of $\mathcal N=4$ SYM on the D3 worldvolume is intimately linked to the type IIB SL(2, Z) duality, which acts on the bulk NSNS and RR fields. 

For comparison, we recall a similar story on the type IIA side.
The analogy is not direct because there is no analogue of the SL(2, Z) duality in the type IIA theory.
The D2 action can be written as \cite{Schmidhuber:1996fy,Tseytlin:1996it}
\begin{equation}
S_{D2}
=\int d^3x \sqrt{-e^{-2\Phi}\det (g_{mn}+(\mathcal F_2)_{mn})}
+\int\left(C_3-C_1\wedge\mathcal F_2\right),\qquad
\mathcal F_2\equiv dA-B_2.
\end{equation}
We dualize the worldvolume vector into a scalar, paralleling the D3 treatment.
We promote $\mathcal F_2$ to an independent field and enforce $\mathcal F_2 = dA-B_2$ through a Lagrange multiplier, here a scalar $y$,
\begin{equation}
\int dy\wedge(\mathcal F_2 + B_2),
\end{equation}
whose variation imposes $d(\mathcal F_2 + B_2)=0$.
Trading the independent $\mathcal F_{mn}$ for its dual vector through $\mathcal F_{mn}=\epsilon_{mnp}v^p$, so that $\tfrac12\mathcal F_{mn}\mathcal F^{mn}=-v_pv^p$, the action becomes
\footnote{In three dimensions the Born--Infeld determinant collapses: for antisymmetric $\mathcal F_{mn}$ one has $\det(g_{mn}+\mathcal F_{mn})=\det g_{mn}(1+\tfrac12\mathcal F_{mn}\mathcal F^{mn})$, so the DBI term equals $e^{-\Phi}\sqrt{-g}\sqrt{1+\tfrac12\mathcal F_{mn}\mathcal F^{mn}}$.}
algebraic in $v$,
\begin{equation}
S_{D2}=\int d^3x\sqrt{-g}\left[e^{-\Phi}\sqrt{1-v_pv^p}-(C_1-dy)_pv^p\right]+\int\left(C_3 + B_2\wedge dy\right).
\end{equation}
Integrating out $v$ using its algebraic equation of motion
\begin{equation}
\frac{e^{-\Phi}v_p}{\sqrt{1-v_qv^q}}=-(C_1-dy)_p,
\label{D2-integrating-out-v}
\end{equation}
We find
\begin{equation}
S_{D2}=\int d^3x\sqrt{-g}\sqrt{e^{-2\Phi}+(C_1-dy)_p(C_1-dy)^p}+\int\left(C_3 + B_2\wedge dy\right).
\end{equation}
Finally, the rank-one identity $\det(a\,g_{mn}+b\,K_mK_n)=a^3\det g_{mn}(1+\tfrac{b}{a}K_pK^p)$ with $a=e^{-2\Phi/3}$, $b=e^{4\Phi/3}$ repackages the integrand into a single determinant,
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
S_{D2}
&=\int d^3 x\sqrt{-\det\big(e^{-2\Phi/3}g_{mn} + e^{4\Phi/3}(C_1-dy)_m(C_1-dy)_n\big)}\\
&\quad+ \int\left(C_3 + B_2\wedge dy\right).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
If one interprets the worldvolume scalar $y$ as a 10d scalar pulled back onto the D2 worldvolume, then this is precisely the M2 action directly reduced on $S_1$ \cite{Bergshoeff:1987cm,Tseytlin:1996it,Schmidhuber:1996fy}: the bracketed metric is the standard Kaluza-Klein form, and $C_3+B_2\wedge dy$ the reduction of the 11d three-form.

Just as the M2 brane reduced on a circle gives the D2, one might hope that in a speculative 12d description the D3 arises from some 3-brane reduced on a two-torus $T^2$.
A two-torus is built from two circles, and its shape --- their relative size and tilt --- is captured by a single complex number $\tau$.
Different choices of which two loops to call the basic cycles are physically equivalent, and passing between them changes $\tau$ by SL(2, Z) transformations such as $\tau\to-1/\tau$, leaving the torus itself untouched.
This is precisely how the SL(2, Z) we found acts on the IIB axio-dilaton, and hence --- through the dualization above --- on the worldvolume gauge coupling.
It is therefore tempting to guess that the axio-dilaton is the shape of a hidden $T^2$, in which case the self-duality we derived by hand would be automatic: nothing more than the freedom to relabel the torus's two cycles.
Whether such a torus is really present is the open question, and a closer look at the D3's degrees of freedom shows why it is not straightforward.
A simple degrees of freedom count suggests that the D3 has enough fields to be embedded in 12d.
However, its two on-shell bosonic degrees of freedom arise from a gauge field, which makes a direct interpretation in terms of embedding coordinates difficult.
One can perform a double dimensional reduction of the D3 so that the vector decomposes into two scalars \cite{Jatkar:1996np,Kar:1997cx}, but this simply reproduces the standard relation between M-theory on $T_2$ and 9d supergravity \cite{Green:1997as}.
